Staten Island is Now the New Hot Spot for COVID-19 in New York City

by Juliet Jeske
12/13/20

In the span of nine months, Staten Island has gone from the borough with the lowest cases of COVID-19 to the highest. Staten Island has only 5% of the population but a death rate 40% higher than any other borough.

Lower population density might play a role. According to a study by John Hopkins University, suburban sprawl, not urban density leads to higher levels of COVID-19 infections and death.

"People in dense places are more likely to take 'stay at home' orders seriously or are more likely to wear masks just because they are more aware of the threat,"said Reid Ewing, co-author of the study.

Public outrage about COVID-19 restrictions might also be a factor. Parents have threatened to sue to open schools, bar owners have openly flouted the law, and public officials have organized anti-COVID-19 restriction rallies as far back as May 20th.
